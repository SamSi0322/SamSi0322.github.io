About

I am a research assistant at the University of Waterloo. My primary research uses large language models and multimodal signals (video, execution traces, code) to automatically debug, tutor, and evaluate block-based programs — 4 first-authored papers including two accepted at FSE 2026 and ISSTA 2026. In parallel, I work on problems in computational number theory. My most recent paper, On the Failure of Multiplicative Mixing in Tao's Collatz Framework (2026), identifies a rigorous obstruction: the multiplicative component of Tao's (2019) affine random process cannot equidistribute beyond logarithmic scale, revealing a structural barrier to strategies that treat multiplicative and additive parts separately. Earlier work produced a research archive on the unit-square rational distance problem. I also have experience in game theory and graph theory, and previously worked at Microsoft Azure & AI Research.
